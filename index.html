About the Font

Atkinson Hyperlegible font is named after J. Robert Atkinson, the founder of the Braille Institute. It has been developed specifically to increase legibility for readers with low vision, and to improve comprehension.

The font distinguishes similar letterforms that are often confused with each other. For example, the letter 'i' has a prominent tail, while '1' has a base serif, making them easily distinguishable.

This typeface is available for free under the SIL Open Font License and can be used in both personal and commercial projects.
